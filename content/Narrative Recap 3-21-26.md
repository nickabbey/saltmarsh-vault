I should begin with what happened between our excursions. After the Zone of Truth concluded and we split up, [[Perrin]] went back to the [[Lizardfolk Queen]] on his own, without telling a soul, and confessed that it was his familiar doing the spying. This earned him a trial by combat with a lizardfolk gladiator from [[Vyth]]'s former tribe.

I know this because while [[Kaldor]], [[Shade]], and I were marching through the marsh on egg duty, I was struck by an astral projection. One moment I was striding through muck; the next I was watching Perrin fight for his life in the throne room. My body, meanwhile, fell face-first into the swamp. Perrin won, barely. The gladiator fought like a man atoning for something.

We rushed back at double speed. I burst in and barked at Perrin to show me his wounds. When I jabbed a bruise and he winced, I was satisfied it had been real. Then I got angry. [[Amos Crane]] was already livid. We had a plan, all of us, and Perrin threw it away without a word. I'll admit I was a little pouty about it. We cooled down and agreed: Amos would diplomatically ask the Queen whether she truly intended to honor her end of the bargain.

After a long rest and a rustic breakfast, I suggested everyone prepare Hold Person. I cast Tongues on Amos and reminded the group to stay close during the casting. The [[Lizardfolk Queen]], Vyth's old tribal leader, and their shaman were assembled. I cast the Zone of Truth and Amos led the questioning.

The tribe admitted to holding Vyth's friend prisoner. They'd beaten him in the past but promised to stop. The man had traded himself to cover some debt of Vyth's; the particulars remain unclear. We negotiated his transfer to the Queen's custody, to be released when the war is won. The Queen reaffirmed her intent to live in peace as allies with [[Saltmarsh]]. Good enough for now.

[[Vyth]] and [[Shade]] went to confirm the prisoner's identity. The man was a shell. Broken in mind, compliant with his captors in every way. Whatever debt was owed, the cost of paying it had been steep.

It then occurred to us that we had committed to a war without knowing the battle plan. We requested an audience with the lizardfolk warchief. Shade, who has seen more wars than the rest of us combined, would lead that conversation. I know when to let a man who knows his business do his work. We await the briefing.
