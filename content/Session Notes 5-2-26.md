## Between sessions

I missed the previous session. The group ran an exhibition combat in town for prizes. [[Shade]] came away with a new sword, its blade covered in runes he cannot read. He asked whether I would cast Comprehend Languages into [[Perrin]]'s Ring of Spell Storing, which he intended to borrow as opportunity allowed, in order to read and transcribe the runework.

Spencer formalized [[Udious]]'s spellbook as a second arcane grimoire +1. I am not attuning it — keeping it as a backup to my primary. Long-term plan: transcribe both books into one another so I have full redundancy across two +1 grimoires.

## Day 1 — Crabber's Cove

[[Shade]] hands me his magical book at the start of session — for no reason that is entirely clear, in or out of character. A tome that records every word spoken or heard in its presence. An excellent addition to my pursuit of knowledge, and one I intend to put to its proper use: a running self-portrait, narrated in the third person, in which Sir Viltevar acquits himself with the dignity and style the occasion demands.

We head out to [[Crabber's Cove]] to disarm the magical wards our scouts found and to formally take the place as our own. A magistrate / real estate broker meets us there to handle the property transfer. A couple of the party read the document over carefully. I practice my signature, making sure that the flourish at the end is grand and impressive. 

We sign the deed. The magistrate leaves.

We talk priorities. The place needs to be made safe. I want to head straight to the tower; the rest of the party is fresh off the tournament and wants something less demanding. We agree to start with the largest building, leftmost on the map — calling it **Building 1** for now, future site of the Inn.

I ask about the vampire. The party shrugs, rubs their necks, and says, *"that's a long story, but it left."* I file this away.

We spend the rest of the day making Building 1 livable as our home base — clearing debris, hauling the wreckage into a burn pile out front for the evening's bonfire.

At sundown we light it, cook, eat. First night on our new HQ.

[[Shade]] teaches me how to catch a crab. I observe aloud that we ought to commission proper crab traps from a carpenter — fresh crab on the menu of our Inn would practically sell itself.

## Day 2 — to the tower

Before sunrise, [[Shade]] knocks on my door. We go out for a run through the coastal brush and the shallows and return a bit before the sun is up. He then shows me the basics of his kata. A pleasant piece of exercise, and unexpectedly familiar — a martial discipline rooted in something close to the tradition our family man-at-arms taught me as a boy.

Over breakfast, we discuss plans for the property. The Inn gets first priority for restoration. I suggest we approach [[Gellan Primewater]] and barter adventuring prowess for guild labor — we keep any spoils from the work he sends us on, he pays us in labor on our buildings. Mixed reactions around the fire, but everyone agrees on the smaller version of the plan: get crab traps commissioned as soon as possible. Passive food for us, anything extra goes to market.

[[Amos Crane|Amos]] leads us to the tower.

## Tower basement — the secret stone fireplace

Through the main chamber and down to the basement. Six steel doors around the hexagonal room. One unlocked from the start. Three warded.

I take the center of the room and cast Detect Magic, confirming the auras: **one necromancy, two abjuration.** [[Perrin]] assists my Arcana check on the necromancy aura. Not a spell I have seen with my own eyes, but I am reasonably confident it is a chronomancy effect — *Tether Essence* — meant to transfer damage recived by one target to another. The targets are randomly selected from those in range the sigil is triggered. I relay this to the group.

When I attempt to dispel it, the necromancy ward goes off. Failing the check, but making the save when the spell fires, I barely evade the effect. It seems the spell never managed to attach its tether to me. The two abjuration wards I dispel cleanly in succession. Both were symbol spells with triggered effects: one would have set an antimagic field on the ceiling; the other fired into the floor too quickly for me to read fully. In both cases I siphoned the weave energy off before it could complete, dissipating it harmlessly.

[[Shade]] and [[Perrin]] then unlock the doors the wards had been guarding.

We agree to investigate the two newly unlocked rooms first (2 and 6), in case more than one threat wakes at the same time.

### Room 2 — the specimen tubes

A row of glass tubes, each filled with liquid, each holding a horror in suspension — hags, [[Sahuagin]], an assortment of beasts and other creatures I do not immediately recognize. A specimen collection.

Each tube has runes etched at its base. The runework reads as an altered, homebrewed(?) binding — closer to stasis than to true imprisonment. Somewhere between Gentle Repose and Feign Death. The cleanest term is **suspended animation**.

The tubes are latched at the bottom and open at the top through a grate. No other doors visible in the room.

I find a desk. No papers on it. None anywhere in the room.

I burn a spell slot to cast Detect Magic and sweep the room properly. Desk first — clean. Then I walk among the tubes: every body inside radiates **necromancy**. The tops of the tubes carry **abjuration**. Someone notices the base of each tube, flush with the floor, looks like a trapdoor with a lock.

[[Amos Crane|Amos]] gasps. One of the bodies in suspension is the very vampire the party had believed had *left*. He had not. He is in here.

We exit Room 2 and lock the door behind us.

### Door 1 — north passageway

We peek through Door 1. It opens onto a passageway: a dug tunnel with wooden bracing, running farther than 60 ft of darkvision can reach. We agree to circle back to it later, and instead turn to **Door 6**.

### Room 6 — scrying chamber

The room resonates with magical energy from all sides — walls, ceiling, and a number of crystalline platforms set at different intervals. The crystal itself is the source of the hum.

Each platform bears a label in a different language, each naming a creature type:

| Platform | Word | Language |
|----------|------|----------|
| (near the door) | humanoid | Common |
| | Dragon | Draconic |
| | Elemental | Primordial |
| | Aberration | Deep Speech |
| | Beast | Druidic |
| | Celestial | Celestial |
| | Undead | Undercommon |
| | Fey | Sylvan |

I touch the Fey platform. Nothing. [[Amos Crane|Amos]] touches it as well — he also speaks Sylvan — and the entire room transforms. We see what looks like the effect of *Scrying* — a floating magical eye's perspective, focused on a dryad Amos once knew.

We understand the chamber for what it is: a scrying array, keyed by creature type and triggered by a speaker of the named language. After Amos's vision, the Fey platform's magical light visibly diminishes — limited charges, not unlimited use.

I step to the Undead platform and try the same, aiming for the vampire in Room 2. The chamber shifts to an uncanny rendering of the room we just left.

We talk through the strategic value of this. I note aloud that it could be put to immediate use spying on the [[Sahuagin]] during the war.

We leave and choose to unlock **Door 3** next — the door that had carried the necromancy ward, so plausibly the next of consequence.

### Perrin departs

Out of nowhere, [[Perrin]] tells us he has received a summons. None of the rest of us heard anything. He is adamant that it is time for him to leave. He hands his **Ring of Spell Storing** to [[Shade]] before he goes.

The timing is strange. He spoke up not long after the necromancy ward had triggered — though I cannot say whether the two are connected. (Dan is switching characters, next week this fight with the stone sentinel will be his last session as perrin. His next character will be a warlock.)

### Door 3 — the stone-like sentinel

We unlock Door 3. Beyond it, a long hallway-like room. At the far end, a large stone-like humanoid sits in front of what looks like a metal chest or strongbox.

I stride confidently forward, hand raised in friendly greeting. *"Ahoy, mate!"* I call.

A click at my feet. A wall drops behind me, separating those of us who pressed ahead from the ones who lagged. The stony figure ahead stirs. He has no intention of returning the greeting.

**Session ends here.**
