Between sessions, after the Zone of Truth concluded, [[Perrin]] went back to the [[Lizardfolk Queen]]on his own and confessed that it was his familiar that had been spying. This triggered a trial by combat with a lizardfolk gladiator, a member of [[Vyth]]''s former tribe. He did this on his own, without telling anyone. I experienced an involuntary astral projection vision of the fight while marching through the marsh on egg duty, collapsing face-first into the  muck. Perrin won the duel narrowly. The gladiator appeared to be atoning for something, and was possibly the prisoner Vyth had been worried about.  
  
 My group rushed back to the [[Lizardfolk Camp]] at double speed. I confirmed the vision was real by inspecting Perrin's wounds. [[Amos Crane]] was furious and I was too, though I was masking worry with anger. and being a little pouty about it. After cooling down, we agreed to proceed with asking the [[Lizardfolk Queen]] about her war plans. Amos would find a diplomatic way to ask whether she intended to double cross us.  
  
 After a long rest and breakfast, we prepared spells (hold person for multiple casters). I cast Tongues on Amos before entering the circle and reminded everyone to stay close during the Zone of Truth casting.  
   
 The Queen, Vyth's old tribal leader, and their shaman were present. Amos led the questioning. The tribe admitted to holding Vyth's friend prisoner and to past beatings, but promised to stop. The prisoner had traded himself to cover a debt of Vyth's (details unclear). We negotiated the prisoner's transfer to the Queen's custody, to be released after the war. The Queen affirmed her intent to honor the alliance and live in peace with [[Saltmarsh]].  
  
 [[Vyth]] and [[Shade]] went to identify the prisoner and confirmed it was the man from the vision. He was broken, compliant, a shell of himself.  
   
 We realized we had no details about the war we'd committed to and requested an audience with the lizardfolk warchief or general. [[Shade]], a veteran of many wars, would lead the military discussion. Session ended there.
