[[Shade]] handled our audience with [[Sauriv (Lizrdfolk Warchief)|Sauriv]], which suited me; he has the most knowledge of war among us. [[Sauriv (Lizrdfolk Warchief)|Sauriv]] advised that they would make contact with [[Saltmarsh Town Council]] in five days, and the first strike would come in ten more days. With that settled, we took our leave a little after lunch and began the march back to where the ships had set us ashore.

We were not long into the journey when we came upon one of the crew, alone on the path, dead. There was nothing to betray the cause of death, though a few of us attempted to discern it. Vexing. He hadn't been dead long, either, which troubled me more than the death itself. [[Vyth]] knelt and cast Spare the Dying upon the poor soul, not to revive him (that was beyond us) but to stay the rot so we might return him home for proper burial, and perhaps some pointed questions once we had him before someone cleverer about corpses than I. Try as we might, none could discern the cause for the whole of the journey.

We pressed on, south along the coast, then cut inland toward the sound. Halfway across we made camp atop a grassy plateau, a fine defensible position, or so I thought.

We placed the body, sewn into a canvas sack, near the fire to deter wild animals from accosting us. Unfortunately, we were set upon by a massacre of owlbears (massacre being, of course, the correct anthropological term for a gathering of such creatures; I make a point of knowing these things). It became quickly apparent that the scent of our unfortunate passenger had carried on the wind and drawn them to us like moths to a lantern. An inconvenient lesson, and one I shall file away for future expeditions involving corpses and the open country. Fortunately, their beaks and claws were no match for our spells and steel.
