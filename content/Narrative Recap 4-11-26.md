We returned from the west, crossing the sound with the help of our druid [[Kaldor]]. A fresh vantage on the town, which I rather enjoyed; every approach reveals something new to the observant eye. We informed the guards at the gate that we would be at [[The Snapping Line]] awaiting the council's summons, and set off into the city.

Our path took us across [[Sharkfin Bridge]]. As I stepped onto it a strange shiver ran the length of my spine, a cold and unpleasant thing I could not place. I filed it away for later consideration and we pressed on.

Food and drink at the Snapping Line were welcome. Two hours passed with no summons from the council (merchants and councilors keeping their own time, as is their habit), so we resolved to put the interval to profitable use and offload our owlbear haul at the tannery. Back across the bridge. And there it was again, the same wretched nausea curling up from beneath my boots. I asked the others whether any of them felt it. None did. Curious. I made a firmer note to return to the question once our business was done.

The tanner was suitably impressed, as well she should have been. The remains of a full massacre of owlbears, held in pristine condition by Gentle Repose within the hour of harvest, presented to her by a knight who knew exactly what he had? The woman had seldom seen such a haul in such a state. We haggled her up to top dollar without much effort. Not bad for what began as an ambush in the dark.

With coin in hand, I called upon [[Captain Xendros]] at the [[Faithful Quartermasters of Iuz]] with a specific purchase in mind. Our next business is [[Crabber's Cove]]; the future site of [[Renegades Headquarters]]. Clearing it means contending with a trapped vampire and a set of magical wards the party noted on its scouting trip. A scroll of Dispel Magic would serve for the latter. I bought it, and then had the further good fortune to find her selling scroll-copying inks and materials, which I purchased on the spot and used to transcribe Dispel Magic into my own book. Six minutes' work. The inks were entirely spent by the end, but knowledge worth having is knowledge worth owning outright.

While there, I raised a separate matter with the party: [[Udious]]'s spellbook, currently sitting idle among our collected effects. I explained, with what I think was uncharacteristic patience, that I was manifestly the superior choice to safeguard such a volume and put its contents to work on the party's behalf. I laid out the logic in clean, measured strokes, the sort of argument that builds of itself once the foundations are properly set. My companions, persuaded by the sheer weight of reason, conceded the point. They saw clearly that attempting to read it themselves would likely cause them actual bodily harm, whereas in my hands it would become a tool of collective benefit. As always, my stunning rhetoric beguiles beings of lesser intellect. The volume is mine.

I will confess, between these pages and no others, that I have been giving thought to a longer game. Keoghland's crown sits heavy on these coastal towns, levying and dictating from a distance while offering rather less protection than its writs pretend. A knight of my standing would be remiss to leave good people laboring indefinitely under such an arrangement. The hidden passage beneath the cove, and our pet [[Thousand Teeth the Devourer|thousand teeth]] as it grows, are well suited to a quieter kind of trade. I have not yet raised the matter with the others. That is a conversation for another day, and for the right moment.

As we prepared to leave, word reached us through [[Kaldor]] that the council was at last ready. We turned back toward the hall. And once again, the bridge.

This time I was watching. I stepped onto it and the nausea returned right on cue. I glanced at my companions. Kaldor, who is a half-elf, looked a bit green about the gills. I asked whether he had felt it, and he gave a shrug and allowed that it happened every time he crossed, that he had grown so used to the sensation he almost no longer noticed it. Almost being the operative word.

I called the party to a halt mid-span and cast Detect Magic. The bridge lit up beneath us in a color I did not care for. A necromancy curse, woven into the stonework itself. I cannot lift curses, but [[Vyth]] may yet be able to. As I was cataloguing the aura, [[Shade]], who has walked more of the world than any of us, noted that the materials the bridge is built from are not native to this region. Imported, then, and cursed besides. An unpleasant combination.

And then [[Vyth]] noticed something else. Something he could not immediately name. We stopped there on the bridge while the matter was considered: a knight and his companions standing on cursed foreign stone in the middle of a town that had no idea what it was walking over every day.
